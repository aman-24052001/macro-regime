Methodology

How It Works — Three Steps

No black boxes. Every decision is traceable to a data point.

📡
Step 1 · Read

Monitor the Economy

12 Federal Reserve (FRED) indicators updated monthly: yield curve shape, credit spreads, unemployment, inflation, industrial output, money supply, and more.

🧠
Step 2 · Classify

Detect the Regime

A 3-state Hidden Markov Model processes the signals and assigns probabilities: Expansion / Stagnation / Contraction. Output is probabilistic — not a binary switch.

⚖️
Step 3 · Rotate

Adjust the Portfolio

Probability-weighted allocation across 6 ETFs (stocks, bonds, gold, credit, cash). Rebalances only when drift exceeds 2% — minimising unnecessary transaction costs.

The Signal Engine

What the Model Reads

12 publicly available government data series from the US Federal Reserve. No proprietary data needed.

📉

Yield Curve Shape

The gap between 10-year and 2-year Treasury yields. When short rates exceed long rates (inversion), it has predicted every US recession since 1970, typically 12–18 months in advance. The single most predictive feature.

💳

Credit Spread (BAA)

The extra yield investors demand to hold corporate bonds vs safe government bonds. Widens sharply when fear rises. Credit markets typically react to recession risk before equity markets do.

💼

Employment Signals

Two measures: Unemployment Rate (monthly) and Initial Jobless Claims (weekly, rolled monthly). Jobless claims spike fast at economic turning points — giving early warning before monthly unemployment data confirms the trend.

🏭

Industrial Output & GDP

Industrial Production (monthly) and Real GDP (quarterly, vintage data). The YoY growth rate captures business cycle momentum. GDP uses ALFRED vintage — the number that was actually published at the time, not later-revised figures.

🔥

Inflation (CPI)

High inflation constrains the Federal Reserve from cutting rates even when the economy slows — the "stagflation" scenario. 2022 is the textbook example: Fed kept hiking even as growth stalled, creating simultaneous losses in stocks and bonds.

🏦

Fed Funds Rate & Money Supply

The Fed's policy rate and M2 money supply growth. Sharp rate-hiking cycles have preceded every major recession since 1980. M2 contraction (rare — only seen in 2022–23 and 1938) signals severe credit tightening.

What Each Metric Tests
Sharpe: Return per unit of total risk. Standard measure at every fund.
Sortino: Return per unit of downside risk only. Better for protection strategies.
Calmar: Return per unit of worst-case loss. CIO/trustee metric.
Omega: Ratio of all gains to all losses. Captures full return distribution.
